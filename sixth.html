Inarizaki High


Inarizaki High is one of Japan’s strongest volleyball teams. They’ve competed in the Spring Tournament 31 times, including the last three years straight. In the latest Interhigh, they placed second and are currently ranked number two nationwide. They defeated Mujinazaka in the semifinals, led by top spiker Wakatsu Kiryū, and even took a set from Itachiyama, the eventual champions.


The year before, Inarizaki finished third in both the Interhigh and Spring High. Their lineup includes standout players like the Miya Twins, Rintarō Suna, and Aran Ojiro, who is considered one of Japan’s top five aces. The team is coached by Norimune Kurosu, a calm and strategic leader known for encouraging bold plays and helping players rise to challenges. He’s respected for balancing flashy talent with grounded discipline, often using psychological tactics like their cheer squad and orchestra to pressure opponents. Their motto, “We don’t need (things like) memories,” reflects their mindset of focusing on the present and chasing new goals.
